Crystallisation

Making pure crystals from a concentrated impure solution

Method

Dissolve the substance in the least volume of hot solvent possible. Leave to cool. Substances are almost always more soluble in a solvent at higher temperature. When the temperature drops to the level were all the substance cannot be dissolved, then crystallisation will happen. Only particles of the same types join a growing crystal. The small amounts of other things don't get to join. The crystals grown are very pure. The other things stay in solution.
